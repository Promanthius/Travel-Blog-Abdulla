The Journey Home (Day 8 - 9)

After a fulfilling tour, it was time for some well-deserved rest and relaxation. Some of us gathered at the cafeteria for drinks and socializing, while others chose to unwind in their rooms. Personally, I opted to step outside to gaze at the night sky. Accompanied by a few others, we found ourselves engrossed in conversations about our hobbies and interests under the twinkling stars. It was a peaceful conclusion to our journey, reflecting on the experiences and bonds formed during our adventure.

The next morning, we prepared for an early departure to NAIA at 6 am. Although my flight wasn't scheduled until the following day, I had a full 24 hours to spend at the airport. Alongside a friend, we leisurely strolled around, occasionally joining our two wonderful teachers who were accompanying us. I felt a twinge of guilt knowing my flight departed before theirs. Eventually, we settled down to rest, cherishing the final moments together.

Upon arriving home after a good hour and a half flight, I was greeted by my family's familiar sarcasm and affectionate remarks, which I had missed dearly even though I had only been away for a week. As I recounted my experiences in Manila and Baguio, sharing tales of adventure and discovery, a profound realization struck me. Amidst all the excitement and new experiences, there truly is no place like home. With heartfelt sincerity, I voiced the thought that had crystallized in my mind: "Nothing's better than home."
