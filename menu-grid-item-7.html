Back to Manila, Baguio (Day 7)

On the Seventh Day, there wasn't much to note as I spent most of my time lazing around in our hotel room until breakfast. I regretted not joining the others on their excursion to the nearby Burnham Park, frustrated with myself for being too lazy to venture out. However, the view from our hotel room was a comforting solace, offering a picturesque scene that was satisfying in its own right. I heard Burnham Park was delightful, with its charming karts, swan rides, and a majestic pond that added to its allure. Despite missing out, I found some contentment in the beauty I could still appreciate from afar.

After breakfast, we began packing up in preparation for our return to Manila by bus. Although the journey had made me weary, I found it impossible to sleep as the views on our way back were exhilarating and beautiful. The dense fog that had enveloped Baguio gradually lifted, revealing the warm colors of sunshine bouncing off the flora we passed by. The lush grasslands, rice paddies, and expansive flatlands that were hidden by the night on our way in were now vividly in sight, showcasing the diverse and breathtaking landscapes of the Philippines. This leg of the journey was a visual feast, making every moment on the road a captivating experience.

As we approached Manila, the transition from Baguio's cool temperatures to the city's intense heat was stark and almost overwhelming. The Manila sun felt like it was burning our skin, a sharp contrast to the pleasant chill we had grown accustomed to in Baguio. This drastic change felt like an equivalent exchange, trading the cool, serene ambiance of the mountains for the bustling, sun-drenched energy of the city. Despite the discomfort, the journey had been a profound and enriching experience, filled with moments of awe and beauty that left an indelible mark on our memories.
